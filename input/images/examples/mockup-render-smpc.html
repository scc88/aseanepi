PAXLOVID film-coated tablets — Summary of Product Characteristics

nirmatrelvir / ritonavir · Summary of Product Characteristics (SmPC)
A deliberately simple render. The same structured ePI Bundle powers the richer interactive showcases on the ePI Pilot page — here it is a plain, readable document with a contents pane and a metadata summary.

1 · Name of the medicinal product

PAXLOVID film-coated tablets.

2 · Qualitative and quantitative composition

Each pink nirmatrelvir film-coated tablet contains 150 mg nirmatrelvir.

Each white to off-white ritonavir film-coated tablet contains 100 mg ritonavir.

For the full list of excipients, see section 6.1.

3 · Pharmaceutical form

3.1 · Nirmatrelvir

Pink, oval film-coated tablet (approximately 17.6 mm × 8.6 mm), debossed with "PFE" on one side and "3CL" on the other.

3.2 · Ritonavir

White to off-white, capsule-shaped film-coated tablet, debossed with "H" on one side and "R9" on the other.

4 · Clinical particulars

4.1 · Therapeutic indications

PAXLOVID is indicated for the treatment of mild-to-moderate coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) in adults who are at high risk of progression to severe COVID-19, including hospitalisation or death.

4.2 · Posology and method of administration

The recommended dose is 300 mg nirmatrelvir (two 150 mg tablets) together with 100 mg ritonavir (one 100 mg tablet), all taken together twice daily for 5 days. Treatment should be started as soon as possible after a diagnosis of COVID-19 has been made and within 5 days of symptom onset.

Renal impairment. Dose adjustment is required according to renal function:

Renal functioneGFR (mL/min)Dose
No / mild impairment≥ 60Nirmatrelvir 300 mg + ritonavir 100 mg (2 + 1) twice daily for 5 days.
Moderate impairment30 to < 60Nirmatrelvir 150 mg + ritonavir 100 mg (1 + 1) twice daily for 5 days. The pack may contain more tablets than needed.
Severe impairment (incl. haemodialysis)< 30Day 1: nirmatrelvir 300 mg + ritonavir 100 mg (2 + 1) once. Days 2–5: nirmatrelvir 150 mg + ritonavir 100 mg (1 + 1) once daily. On dialysis days, take the dose after haemodialysis.

Method of administration. The tablets should be swallowed whole and may be taken with or without food. PAXLOVID is not recommended in patients with severe hepatic impairment and has not been studied in patients younger than 18 years of age.

4.3 · Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to the active substances or to any of the excipients listed in section 6.1.

PAXLOVID is contraindicated with medicinal products that are highly dependent on CYP3A for clearance and for which elevated concentrations are associated with serious and/or life-threatening reactions, and with potent CYP3A inducers where significantly reduced nirmatrelvir/ritonavir concentrations may lead to loss of virologic response. Examples include:

  • Alfuzosin
  • Ranolazine
  • Amiodarone
  • Colchicine
  • Lurasidone
  • Ergot derivatives
  • Lovastatin, simvastatin
  • Sildenafil (for pulmonary arterial hypertension)
  • Oral midazolam, triazolam
  • Rifampin
  • Carbamazepine
  • St John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum)

4.4 · Special warnings and precautions for use

  • Serious adverse reactions due to drug interactions. Concomitant use with certain medicinal products may lead to potentially significant drug interactions (see sections 4.3 and 4.5).
  • Hypersensitivity, anaphylaxis and serious skin reactions. Anaphylaxis and serious skin reactions have been reported; discontinue if signs or symptoms occur and initiate appropriate treatment.
  • Hepatotoxicity. Caution should be exercised when administering PAXLOVID to patients with pre-existing liver disease, liver enzyme abnormalities or hepatitis.
  • Risk of HIV-1 resistance. Ritonavir may lead to development of HIV-1 resistance in individuals with uncontrolled or undiagnosed HIV-1 infection.

4.6 · Fertility, pregnancy and lactation

Pregnancy should be avoided during treatment and for 7 days after completing treatment with PAXLOVID. Ritonavir may reduce the efficacy of combined hormonal contraceptives; patients using such contraceptives should use an additional or alternative non-hormonal method during treatment and until the end of one menstrual cycle after stopping PAXLOVID. Breast-feeding should be discontinued during treatment and for 48 hours after the last dose.

4.7 · Effects on ability to drive and use machines

The effects of PAXLOVID on the ability to drive and use machines have not been specifically studied.

4.9 · Overdose

There is no specific antidote for overdose with PAXLOVID. Management consists of general supportive measures, including monitoring of the clinical status of the patient.

5 · Pharmacological properties

5.1 · Pharmacodynamic properties

Nirmatrelvir is an inhibitor of the SARS-CoV-2 main protease (Mpro, also known as 3C-like protease or 3CLpro), which is required for viral replication. Ritonavir is a CYP3A inhibitor with no anti–SARS-CoV-2 activity; it is co-administered to slow the metabolism of nirmatrelvir and thereby boost its plasma concentrations to the target therapeutic range. In the EPIC-HR clinical trial, PAXLOVID reduced the relative risk of COVID-19–related hospitalisation or death by approximately 86% compared with placebo in high-risk adults treated within the first days of symptom onset.

5.2 · Pharmacokinetic properties

Following oral administration, nirmatrelvir reaches maximum plasma concentrations (Tmax) at approximately 3 hours, with an elimination half-life (t½) of about 6 hours. Food increases nirmatrelvir exposure modestly. Nirmatrelvir is approximately 69% bound to plasma proteins and is eliminated primarily by renal excretion, whereas ritonavir is eliminated mainly by the hepatobiliary route.

5.3 · Preclinical safety data

Nirmatrelvir was not genotoxic in a standard battery of assays and had no effect on fertility in animal studies. Reproductive-toxicity findings were observed only at high systemic exposures. Non-clinical data reveal no special hazard for humans at clinically relevant exposures.

6 · Pharmaceutical particulars

6.1 · List of excipients

Nirmatrelvir tablets — core: colloidal silicon dioxide, croscarmellose sodium, lactose monohydrate, microcrystalline cellulose, sodium stearyl fumarate. Coating: hydroxypropyl methylcellulose, iron oxide red, polyethylene glycol, titanium dioxide.

Ritonavir tablets — core: copovidone, sorbitan laurate, silica colloidal anhydrous (E551), calcium hydrogen phosphate, sodium stearyl fumarate. Coating: hypromellose (E464), titanium dioxide (E171), macrogol (E1521), hydroxypropyl cellulose (E463), talc (E553b), silica colloidal anhydrous (E551), polysorbate 80 (E433).

6.2 · Incompatibilities

Not applicable.

6.3 · Shelf life

Refer to the outer carton.

6.4 · Special precautions for storage

Store at or below 30 °C.

6.5 · Nature and contents of container

Carton of 30 tablets presented as 5 daily-dose blister cards. Each daily card contains 4 nirmatrelvir tablets and 2 ritonavir tablets, arranged as morning and evening doses (marked with sun and moon symbols).

6.6 · Special precautions for disposal

Any unused medicinal product or waste material should be disposed of in accordance with local requirements.
