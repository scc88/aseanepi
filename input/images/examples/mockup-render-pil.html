Read all of this leaflet carefully before you start taking this medicine because it contains important information for you.

  • Keep this leaflet. You may need to read it again.
  • If you have any further questions, ask your doctor or pharmacist.
  • This medicine has been prescribed for you only. Do not pass it on to others. It may harm them, even if their signs of illness are the same as yours.
  • If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet. See section 4.

1 · What PAXLOVID is and what it is used for

PAXLOVID contains two active substances, nirmatrelvir and ritonavir. It is an antiviral medicine used to treat mild-to-moderate coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) in adults who are at high risk of their illness becoming severe.

PAXLOVID works by stopping the virus that causes COVID-19 from multiplying in your body. This can help your body fight the infection and may keep your illness from getting worse.

Talk to your doctor if you do not feel better, or if you feel worse, after 5 days.

2 · What you need to know before you take PAXLOVID

Do not take PAXLOVID

Do not take PAXLOVID if you are allergic to nirmatrelvir, ritonavir, or any of the other ingredients of this medicine (listed in section 6). Also do not take PAXLOVID if you are taking certain other medicines, because it can cause serious or life-threatening effects when combined with them. These include, for example:

  • alfuzosin (used for an enlarged prostate)
  • colchicine (used for gout)
  • certain medicines for heart-rhythm problems, such as amiodarone
  • simvastatin or lovastatin (used to lower cholesterol)
  • ergot medicines for migraine (such as ergotamine, dihydroergotamine)
  • sildenafil, when used for pulmonary arterial hypertension (high blood pressure in the lungs)
  • midazolam or triazolam taken by mouth (used to help you sleep)
  • rifampin (an antibiotic for tuberculosis)
  • carbamazepine (used for epilepsy)
  • the herbal remedy St John's wort (Hypericum perforatum)

This is an excerpt for illustration — the full list of medicines you must not take with PAXLOVID is captured in the structured ePI.

Warnings and precautions

Talk to your doctor or pharmacist before taking PAXLOVID if any of the following applies to you:

  • Allergic reactions. Serious allergic reactions (including anaphylaxis) have happened in people taking PAXLOVID, sometimes even after only one dose. Stop taking PAXLOVID and get medical help straight away if you have trouble breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue or throat, tightness in the throat, or a skin rash.
  • Liver disease. Tell your doctor if you have any liver problems.
  • Kidney disease. Tell your doctor if you have any kidney problems, as your dose may need to be adjusted (see section 3).
  • Untreated HIV. If you have HIV-1 infection that is not being treated, taking PAXLOVID may increase the risk that the HIV virus becomes resistant to some HIV medicines.
  • Children and adolescents. PAXLOVID is not for use in anyone under 18 years of age.

Other medicines and PAXLOVID

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking, have recently taken or might take any other medicines. Many medicines can interact with PAXLOVID. Keep a list of all the medicines you take and show it to your doctor or pharmacist. Do not start taking a new medicine while on PAXLOVID without first telling your doctor or pharmacist.

Pregnancy and breast-feeding

Avoid becoming pregnant while taking PAXLOVID and for 7 days after your last dose. If you use hormonal contraception (such as the pill), use an additional or alternative non-hormonal method of contraception during this time. Do not breast-feed while taking PAXLOVID and for 48 hours after your last dose. Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking this medicine if you are pregnant, think you may be pregnant, or are breast-feeding.

Driving and using machines

The effects of PAXLOVID on the ability to drive and use machines have not been specifically studied.

PAXLOVID contains lactose

PAXLOVID tablets contain lactose. If you have been told by your doctor that you have an intolerance to some sugars, contact your doctor before taking this medicine.

3 · How to take PAXLOVID

Always take this medicine exactly as your doctor or pharmacist has told you. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.

A course of PAXLOVID lasts 5 days. Swallow the tablets whole with water. Do not chew, break or crush them. You can take PAXLOVID with or without food.

The recommended dose depends on your kidney function:

Your kidneysEach doseHow often
No or mild kidney disease2 pink nirmatrelvir tablets + 1 white ritonavir tablet (taken together)Twice a day for 5 days
Moderate kidney disease1 pink nirmatrelvir tablet + 1 white ritonavir tablet (taken together)Twice a day for 5 days (your pack may contain more tablets than you need)
Severe kidney diseaseDay 1: 2 pink + 1 white once. Days 2–5: 1 pink + 1 white once dailyOnce a day

If you forget to take a dose: if you remember within 8 hours of the time it was due, take it as soon as you remember. If more than 8 hours have passed, skip the missed dose and take your next dose at the usual time. Do not take a double dose to make up for a forgotten one.

Do not stop taking PAXLOVID without talking to your doctor first, even if you feel better.

4 · Possible side effects

Like all medicines, this medicine can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.

Common (may affect up to 1 in 10 people)

  • an altered sense of taste
  • headache
  • diarrhoea
  • feeling sick (nausea)

Uncommon (may affect up to 1 in 100 people)

  • allergic reactions (such as itching or a rash)
  • increased blood pressure
  • being sick (vomiting)
  • abdominal (tummy) pain

Rare (may affect up to 1 in 1,000 people)

  • a serious allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
  • feeling generally unwell
  • a serious skin reaction (Stevens-Johnson syndrome or toxic epidermal necrolysis)

Reporting of side effects

If you get any side effects, tell your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet. By reporting side effects you can help provide more information on the safety of this medicine.

5 · How to store PAXLOVID

  • Keep this medicine out of the sight and reach of children.
  • Do not use this medicine after the expiry date shown on the pack.
  • Store at or below 30 °C.
  • Do not refrigerate or freeze.

6 · Contents of the pack and other information

What PAXLOVID contains

The active substances are:

  • Nirmatrelvir 150 mg — the pink tablet.
  • Ritonavir 100 mg — the white tablet.

The other ingredients include lactose and other tablet-forming and coating excipients (see "PAXLOVID contains lactose" in section 2).

What PAXLOVID looks like and contents of the pack

Each pack contains 30 tablets arranged as 5 daily-dose blister cards. The nirmatrelvir tablets are pink and oval, marked "PFE" on one side and "3CL" on the other. The ritonavir tablets are white and capsule-shaped, marked "H" on one side and "R9" on the other.

Product owner

Pfizer Inc., New York, US.
